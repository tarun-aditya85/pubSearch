Key Assumptions & Risks
What could break this math:

Willingness to pay: Researchers are notoriously cheap (many survive on free tools)
Institutional buyers: Moving upmarket to university/pharma licenses could 10X revenue but requires sales team
Competitive moats: Many of these have existing players (Mendeley, Scholarcy, etc.) - you need differentiation
Regulatory: Patent/clinical trial data has licensing costs; compliance tools need legal review
What could accelerate this:

Bundling: Offer "Research Suite" at $49/month (includes 5-6 features) vs. à la carte
Freemium viral loops: Free citation manager → paid team workspaces → enterprise compliance
API/integration revenue: Let other tools (Roam, Notion, Obsidian) pay for your data layer
Bottom line: You're not just selling search - you're selling research productivity infrastructure. The real money is in solving the 10 steps before and after the search.
